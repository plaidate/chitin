# Fightin' Chitin — Player's Manual

## The idea

Two bugs enter, one bug leaves. Fights are **best of 3 rounds**; win a round by
draining the other bug's health or by having more health when the timer runs
out. Two attack buttons and the d-pad carry the basics; motion inputs and the
**crank** unlock everything deeper.

## Controls

| Input | Action |
|---|---|
| **✛ ← / →** | Walk toward / away. Hold *away* from the foe to **block**. Double-tap = **dash / back-hop**. |
| **✛ ↓** | Crouch (low block, low attacks). |
| **✛ ↑ / ↖ / ↗** | Jump (neutral / back / forward). |
| **Ⓐ** | **Light** attack — fast, chains into combos. |
| **Ⓑ** | **Heavy** attack — slow, big damage, knocks down. |
| **Ⓐ + Ⓑ** | **Throw** in close (beats block). |
| **Crank** | Charge your **Frenzy** meter (and, snapped, **parry** — see below). |

## Special moves

Specials are a **motion then an attack button**. Each has a Light (Ⓐ) and Heavy
(Ⓑ) version. The four motions:

- **↓ ↘ →** — quarter-circle forward
- **→ ↓ ↘** — "dragon punch" (Z-motion), usually an anti-air reversal
- **charge ← (hold ~1s) then →** — charge move, rewards defensive play
- **360°** (a full d-pad circle) + **Ⓐ+Ⓑ** — command grab (grapplers)

Every fighter has two specials and a Super; see the roster below.

## The crank — Frenzy, Super, Molt, Parry

- **Frenzy:** spin the crank to fill your Frenzy gauge (fights fill it slowly
  too). While you're actively cranking you can't dash and your block leaks chip
  damage — building meter is a risk you take in an opening.
- **Super:** at a full bar, **↓↘→ + Ⓐ+Ⓑ** unleashes your fighter's Super —
  invulnerable on startup, big damage, empties the bar.
- **Molt** (comeback): at **low health** with a **full bar**, **double-tap ↓ +
  Ⓐ+Ⓑ**. You shed your exoskeleton — heal a chunk, then enter a few seconds of
  **teneral rage** (faster, hit harder) at the cost of taking extra damage while
  soft. Once per match.
- **Parry:** a **fast crank snap** in the instant before a hit lands negates it,
  throws the attacker into recovery, and feeds you Frenzy. Tight window, high
  reward.

## The roster

| Fighter | Archetype | Specials (Light / Heavy) | Super |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rhinoceros Beetle** | Grappler — high HP, slow, armor | Gore Charge (charge) · Pry-&-Flip (360 grab) | Boulder Toss |
| **Leaf-footed Bug** | Technical grappler | Leg-Hook (360) · Thorn Counter (↓↙← — counters & reflects) | Bramble Suplex |
| **Praying Mantis** | Rushdown — low HP, deadly | Strike Rush (↓↘→) · Overhead Reap (→↓↘) | Prayer's End |
| **Tiger Beetle** | Speed rushdown | Blur Dash (↓↘→) · Mandible Flurry (↓↙←, mashable) | Blur Storm |
| **Dragonfly** | Aerial — can fly | Wing Buffet (↓↘→ projectile, air-OK) · Dogfight Dive (→↓↘) | Hawking Run |
| **Assassin Bug** | Zoner — long reach, poison | Venom Jab (↓↘→, poisons) · Rostrum Spear (charge) | Liquefy |
| **Army Ant Major** | Boss / unlockable | tougher; swarm attacks | Swarm |

**Flight (Dragonfly):** hold **↑** to gain and hold altitude, move freely in the
air, and air-dash; you have limited air-time that recharges on the ground. Her
aerials work while flying.

**Poison (Assassin Bug):** Venom Jab and Liquefy leave a **damage-over-time**
that keeps ticking — it can even finish a fight after the hit.

## Modes

- **Arcade** — pick a fighter, climb a ladder of the other five (each on their
  home stage), then face the **Army Ant Major**. Clear it for your fighter's
  ending — and to **unlock the Army Ant** as a playable pick.
- **Survival** — an endless gauntlet; health partly carries between fights.
- **Time Attack** — clear the ladder against the clock.
- **Training** — pick both sides, infinite time, a practice dummy.
- **Versus** — a single match against the CPU.

Music can be muted from the Playdate **system menu**.

## Tips

- Blocking costs nothing but ground — but throws beat block, so don't just sit.
- Save a full Frenzy bar for a clutch **Molt** when you're about to lose.
- Anti-air jump-ins with a dragon-punch special (→↓↘).
- Against the Dragonfly, punish her when she has to land to recharge.
- Against the Assassin Bug, don't trade — chip poison can win the race for her.
- The parry is scary but real: a well-timed crank snap flips a whole round.
